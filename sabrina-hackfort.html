The Hackfort Equation

Quantum Criticality Index

A mathematical framework derived from a single axiom — the Hackfort Axiom — quantifying the degree to which quantum effects dominate a system's behavior. The equation unifies quantum biology, pharmacology, and cryptographic security under one principle: classical models of quantum systems are systematically incomplete, and the incompleteness is quantifiable.

ΦH(S, t) = Σi wi · αi(t) · ηi · (1 + |λ̃i(t)|) where ηi = 1 − 1/κi  ·  κi = kQM/kclass  ·  λ̃i = (d/dt ln Γi) / λref

Proven properties: non-negative, dimensionless, bounded, with a complete formal proof. Falsifiable predictions for enzyme kinetics under inhibition, biodiversity loss quantification, and cryptographic vulnerability assessment.
